About herself, Sonia wrote that she had succeeded in making some
acquaintances in the town, that she did sewing, and, as there was scarcely a
dressmaker in the town, she was looked upon as an indispensable person in
many houses. But she did not mention that the authorities were, through her,
interested in Raskolnikov; that his task was lightened and so on.
At last the news came (Dounia had indeed noticed signs of alarm and
uneasiness in the preceding letters) that he held aloof from everyone, that his
fellow prisoners did not like him, that he kept silent for days at a time and was
becoming very pale. In the last letter Sonia wrote that he had been taken very
seriously ill and was in the convict ward of the hospital.
